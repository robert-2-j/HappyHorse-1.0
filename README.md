# what is HappyHorse 1.0

## At a Glance

As of **April 9, 2026**, the strongest public evidence around `HappyHorse 1.0` comes from `@ArtificialAnlys`.

- `HappyHorse 1.0` appeared as a **pseudonymous video model** in the Artificial Analysis video arena.
- It reached **#1** in **Text-to-Video / Image-to-Video (No Audio)**.
- It reached **#2** in the corresponding **With Audio** ranking.
- Artificial Analysis also published example images and side-by-side comparison videos against `Dreamina Seedance 2.0`, `Kling 3.0 Pro`, `grok-video-imagine`, and `PixVerse V6`.

What remains unclear is the identity of the team behind it. The claims tying it to **Alibaba / Taotian Group** and an eventual **open-source** release spread quickly, but were not publicly confirmed by Alibaba-affiliated official accounts at that point.

## Source Hierarchy

### Tier A: Primary, directly citable sources

- `@Physalis20` explicitly pushed back on the Alibaba plus open-source narrative and stressed that no official public confirmation had appeared.

## Confirmed Facts vs Unconfirmed Claims

### Confirmed

- `HappyHorse 1.0` entered the public conversation through `@ArtificialAnlys`.
- It ranked extremely high in the Artificial Analysis video arena.
- Artificial Analysis released images and multiple comparison videos featuring the model.
- The model quickly became the subject of community comparisons and commentary.

### Plausible, but still not publicly confirmed by official accounts

- `HappyHorse 1.0` came from a Chinese team.
- `HappyHorse 1.0` was tied to Alibaba or Taotian Group.
- `HappyHorse 1.0` would be open-source.

### Why Alibaba Should Not Be Presented as Confirmed

Reverse checks around `HappyHorse` surfaced ranking and samples from `@ArtificialAnlys`, but no relevant public confirmation from `@Alibaba_Cloud` or `@QwenLM`. That leaves the ownership question unresolved.

## Working Summary

The safest concise formulation is:

> `HappyHorse 1.0` emerged on April 8, 2026 as a pseudonymous video generation model introduced by Artificial Analysis. It immediately attracted attention by taking the top spot in the no-audio video rankings and by holding up well in comparison clips against leading competitors. At the same time, a fast-moving rumor cycle formed around the idea that the model came from Alibaba or Taotian Group and might be open-source. As of April 9, 2026, that identity claim remained widely discussed but not publicly confirmed by official Alibaba-affiliated accounts.
